About Me

I am a student at Harvey Mudd College pursuing a double major in mathematics and computer science with a humanities concentration in the history of science. I enjoy mathematics, coding, puzzles, history, and weightlifting.

Within mathematics, I'm interested in applied combinatorics and algorithms. Currently I'm geeking out about the Greedy Matroid Theorem because it explains so many algorithms I've seen in the past few years. It also provides good after-the-fact motivation for my research on phased matroids.

I like putting my math into code because doing so makes it feel more useful. Being able to desribe the generating function for the possible scores of a dice game is cool, but it's even cooler to code it up, get the numbers, and use a matrix game solver to determine the optimal strategy.

I'm also into the history of science, particularly the scientific history of sexuality. In spring of 2014, I did research with professor Vivien Hamilton (Harvey Mudd College) on gendered conceptions of sadism and masochism during the fin de siècle medicalization of sex.
